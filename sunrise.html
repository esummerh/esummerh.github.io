Capstone Project - Sunrise

Sunrise is a trauma-informed mobile application designed to support individuals navigating experiences of violence, abuse, or personal crisis. Built with privacy, discretion, and emotional safety at its core, the app includes secure journaling, anonymous support forums, emergency contact access, and curated resource directories—all integrated into a calming, non-intrusive interface.

Developed during a senior capstone course at the University of Michigan, Sunrise was created by a cross-disciplinary team of five students with backgrounds in computer science, user experience design, and trauma-informed research. Our team collaborated using tools like Figma, Jira, GitHub, and Xcode to plan, prototype, and implement the application.

Throughout the semester, we focused on creating a digital space that prioritizes safety, empowerment, and usability. Every feature was thoughtfully designed and tested with a strong emphasis on accessibility, anonymity, and intuitive interaction—ensuring Sunrise meets the needs of users in vulnerable situations.

Discreet Interface

Sunrise is intentionally designed to protect the user's privacy and safety. Its interface is intentionally subtle, with no overt reference to abuse, violence, or trauma. This ensures that even if someone glances at the screen, they won't be able to infer the app's true intent - making it safe to use in sensitive or dangerous environments.

  • Camouflaged as a sunrise tracking app
  • Neutral wording and icons
  • Soft and non-threatening visuals
  • Authenticated user login

Multimedia Journal

The journal feature empowers users to document their experiences in a secure and deeply personal way. Designed to support emotional reflection and safety documentation, this tool accepts a variety of input types while keeping entries private. It's an essential outlet for users navigating trauma, offering a way to process feelings without fear of exposure.

  • Calendar view makes past entries accessible
  • Voice memos
  • Photo attachments

Peer Support Forum

The forum feature fosters safe, anonymous conversation spaces where users can find comfort, guidance, and solidarity from others who share similar lived experiences. Built with user privacy and safety in mind, if offers a nonjudgmental environment for connection and healing.

  • Anonymous posting
  • Moderated for safety
  • Search and filter tools
  • Saved discussions

Risk Assessment

The assessment feature offers a private, reflective tool for users to evaluate their current safety and well-being. It is designed to help users identify red flags and understand the level of risk they may be facing in their environment. Based on the results, users are gently encouraged to seek support, without triggering emotional distress.

  • Visual score feedback
  • Reassess capabilities
  • Behavioral and situational prompts
  • Supportive suggestions

Local Resources

The resources feature empowers users to quickly find relevant support based on their current location. Whether someone needs emergency housing, legal aid, or mental health services, this tools delivers nearby, reliable resources.

  • Use of location data
  • Search by category
  • Easily accessible contact information
  • Curated for safety
